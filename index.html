THE BLUR
IS THE
SUBJECT

I work exclusively in black and white. Not out of nostalgia, but because colour is noise — it seduces the eye before the eye has time to think. Strip it away and what remains is geometry, light, and the weight of a moment.

My practice centres on motion: intentional camera movement, long exposures, radical defocus. The camera becomes a brush. Sharpness is overrated — the human eye in real life never resolves everything at once.

I shoot on 35mm and 120mm film, developed in my Zürich studio. Every print is hand-made. Limited editions only.

PROCESS

How the work is made
01

EXPOSURE

Every shot is planned around light and movement. I use ND filters, long shutter speeds, and deliberate camera motion to build the frame over time rather than freeze it.

02

FILM

Ilford HP5 and Kodak TMax are my films of choice. I push them 1–2 stops for grain structure. The negative is the first edit — everything after is refinement.

03

DARKROOM

Developed and printed in my Zürich studio. I use fibre-based paper for permanence. Dodging and burning in the enlarger, not in Lightroom.

04

EDITION

All prints are limited to 10 + 2AP. Each is signed, numbered, and accompanied by a certificate. Once an edition is sold out, it is never reprinted.
