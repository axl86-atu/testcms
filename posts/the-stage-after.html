The Stage After

The performance ended thirty years ago. The spotlight was still on when I arrived. I have no explanation for that and I stopped looking for one.

On empty performance spaces

There is a theatre in the outskirts of Lyon that closed in 1987. The seats are still there. The curtain is still there, half drawn, caught mid-gesture like a sentence that stopped before the verb. Someone left a single spotlight running — or it came on by itself, which is the version I prefer — and when I arrived at 6am with a tripod and a permission letter that may or may not have been legitimate, it was illuminating a circle of empty stage approximately two metres in diameter. I set up inside that circle and pointed the camera at the seats.
"Theatre is a verb before it is a noun, an act before it is a place." — Martha Graham
